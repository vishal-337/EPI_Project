Opioid Epidemic Network Modeling

Spatial Diffusion of High-Prescribing Behaviors Across States and Counties
Rishi Bandi & Vishal Maradana
Introduction

The US Opioid Crisis claimed over 500,000 lives (1999-2019). Traditional approaches treat regions as independent units, missing a critical mechanism: spatial diffusion.

Research Question: How does high-prescribing behavior spread across geographic boundaries?

Hypothesis: Opioid prescribing patterns diffuse spatially through neighbor influence, similar to a contagion.

Methodology

1. Network Science Framework

  • Nodes: States/Counties
  • Edges: Geographic adjacency
  • Infection: Rate > 87.35 (75th percentile)

2. SAR Model

Rate(t+1) = β×Rate(t) + γ×Neighbor_Rate(t)

0.981

Path Dependence (β)

0.009

Neighbor Spillover (γ)

Why SAR? Unlike standard regression, SAR captures spatial autocorrelation, validating that geography drives the epidemic.

Conclusions & Future Work
  • Geography matters: Spatial proximity drives diffusion (R²=0.884).
  • Predictive Power: Network models forecast hotspots 5-6x better than random.
  • Policy: Regional coordination is required; single-state laws fail due to network redundancy.
